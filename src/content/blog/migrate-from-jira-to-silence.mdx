Migrating from Jira to Silence Projects is simpler than you think. This guide walks you through the full process — from exporting your Jira data to running your first sprint in Silence.

## Before you start

You'll need:

- Jira admin access (for export)
- Silence admin access
- ~30 minutes

## Step 1: Export from Jira

In Jira, go to **Settings → System → Backup Manager**. Download a full backup in XML format. This includes projects, issues, comments, and attachments.

Alternatively, use the Jira CSV export for individual projects: **Board → Project settings → Export**.

## Step 2: Import into Silence

In Silence, navigate to **Admin → Data Import → Jira**. Upload your XML backup or CSV file. Silence maps Jira concepts to Silence equivalents automatically:

| Jira | Silence Projects |
|------|-----------------|
| Epic | Epic |
| Story | Task |
| Bug | Bug |
| Sprint | Sprint |
| Component | Module tag |

Custom fields, labels, and assignees are preserved where a matching user exists in your Silence workspace.

## Step 3: Review and adjust

After import, review your boards. You may want to:

- Adjust workflow statuses to match your team's process
- Set up automations (e.g., notify on status change)
- Connect issues to your CRM deals or customer accounts

## Step 4: Invite your team

Add your team members from **Admin → Users**. They'll receive a welcome email with onboarding resources.

## That's it

Most teams are fully productive in Silence Projects within a single working day. The Jira muscle memory translates — boards, sprints, backlogs and filters all work the way you expect, just faster and without the per-seat bill.
